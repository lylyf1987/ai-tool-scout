CoCounsel — The AI Legal Co-Pilot

CoCounsel by Casetext (now part of Thomson Reuters) is the most capable AI legal assistant. It can research legal questions, prepare for depositions, draft correspondence, analyze contracts, and create timelines — all with proper legal citations.

Using ChatGPT for Legal Work

ChatGPT is useful for legal professionals as a drafting and brainstorming tool. Use it to draft initial versions of legal documents, brainstorm legal arguments, summarize lengthy documents, and create client communication templates. Always verify output against authoritative legal sources.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI tool for lawyers?

CoCounsel by Thomson Reuters (powered by GPT-4) is the best AI legal assistant. Harvey AI is best for large law firms. Clio is best for legal practice management with AI features.

Can AI do legal research?

Yes, AI legal research tools like CoCounsel, Casetext, and Westlaw AI can search case law, summarize relevant precedents, identify legal arguments, and draft research memos in minutes instead of hours.

Is AI replacing lawyers?

AI is not replacing lawyers but augmenting their capabilities. AI handles document review, legal research, and contract analysis while lawyers focus on strategy, client counseling, courtroom advocacy, and complex legal judgment.

Can AI draft legal documents?

Yes, AI can draft contracts, briefs, memos, and standard legal documents. Always have AI-generated legal documents reviewed by a licensed attorney to ensure accuracy, compliance, and proper legal language.

How much do AI legal tools cost?

AI legal tools range from free (ChatGPT for basic research) to $100-500/month for professional platforms like CoCounsel and Clio. Enterprise solutions for large firms cost significantly more.
